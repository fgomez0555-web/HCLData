How the Homeless Concern Line works
Every request follows a four-step care pathway from intake to outcome
1
Request received
Community members, City staff, Safety Ambassadors, or individuals experiencing homelessness contact the line.
2
Triage & dispatch
Requests are reviewed and routed to the right outreach team based on presenting need and time of day.
3
Field response
Outreach teams engage the individual, assess needs, and provide services, referrals, or care coordination on-scene.
4
Outcome documented
Each interaction is logged — services accepted, referrals made, and cases where individuals could not be located.
Who responds
Two outreach teams operate 24/7 with defined lead roles by time of day
Day lead — 7 AM to 7 PM
Healthcare in Action (HIA)
Backup support available overnight
Focus: Medical care, behavioral health, and ongoing client engagement for individuals with complex health and housing needs.
Night lead — 7 PM to 7 AM
WeHo Care Team (Sycamores)
Backup support available during daytime
Focus: Behavioral health response, de-escalation, and social service linkage for overnight and weekend needs.

Key takeaways
West Hollywood's care-first coordination hub The HCL is the single point of contact for all non-emergency homelessness-related service requests, coordinating 993 responses across nine months.
Demand grew steadily and significantly The daily request rate rose from 2.2 requests/day in August 2025 to 7.6/day in February 2026 — a 3.5× increase — as community awareness and use of the line grew throughout the year.
Service-oriented, not enforcement-driven Over 84% of requests involve housing, health, or welfare concerns. The system is built to connect people with care — not enforcement.
Fast response, persistent engagement 73% of teams arrived within 15 minutes. Of those successfully reached, 63% accepted services. Outreach continues for individuals who declined or could not be located.
